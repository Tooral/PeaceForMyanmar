Protests in Myanmar, known locally as the Spring Revolution began in early 2021 in opposition to the coup d'état on 1 February, staged by Min Aung Hlaing, the commander-in-chief of the country's armed forces, the Tatmadaw. As of 11 April 2021, at least 706 civilians of which at least 44 were children, have been killed by military or police forces and at least 3,059 people detained.
